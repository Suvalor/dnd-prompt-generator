Privacy Policy

Effective and last updated June 15, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how DND Prompt Forge, operated through whatai.me ("DND Prompt Forge," "we," "us," or "our"), collects, uses, discloses, and protects information when you visit dnd.whatai.me or use our prompt-generation services (collectively, the "Service").

1. Scope

This Policy applies to information processed through the Service. It does not apply to third-party websites, image-generation services, advertisements, or other services that you may access through links or use after copying a prompt. Those services operate under their own privacy policies.

2. Information we collect

Information you provide

  • Prompt content. Character, creature, scene, style, equipment, and other details you enter to generate a text prompt.
  • Feedback. A request identifier, rating, selected feedback reasons, and any optional comment you submit.
  • Communications. Your email address and the contents of messages you send to our support address.

Information collected automatically

  • Device and connection data. IP address, browser and device characteristics, language, screen dimensions, time zone, and similar technical information.
  • Service activity. Request identifiers, prompt type, selected model, request status, timing, quota usage, and error information.
  • Cookies and similar identifiers. A signed anonymous session cookie and a browser-characteristic hash used for security and rate limiting.
  • Advertising data on the homepage. When you visit the homepage, Google and its advertising partners may receive the page URL, IP address, cookie or device identifiers, and information about ad interactions.

We do not require an account, payment details, or a legal name to use the Service. Please do not enter personal, confidential, financial, health, or other sensitive information into prompt or feedback fields.

3. Sources of information

We collect information directly from you, automatically from your browser or device, from our hosting and security infrastructure, and from service providers that help us operate prompt generation and homepage advertising.

4. How we use information

We may use information to:

  • provide, operate, and improve prompt generation;
  • process requests through our server or an OpenAI-compatible AI service provider;
  • provide a local template-based result when online generation is unavailable;
  • enforce request limits and protect against fraud, abuse, and security threats;
  • diagnose errors, monitor reliability, and maintain audit records;
  • review feedback and improve prompt-generation rules;
  • respond to support and privacy requests;
  • display, measure, and support advertising on the homepage; and
  • comply with law and protect our rights, users, and the public.

5. Prompt processing and AI providers

When online generation is available, prompt details are sent to our server and may be transmitted to the OpenAI-compatible AI provider configured for the Service. That provider may process information under its own terms and privacy practices. If online generation is unavailable, the browser may create a template-based prompt locally. Do not submit information that you do not want processed by an AI service provider.

6. Cookies and similar technologies

Our signed anonymous session cookie supports request authentication, security, and rate limiting and may remain on your device for up to 10 days. The Service may also generate a hash from browser and device characteristics for rate limiting. You may block or delete cookies in your browser, but doing so can prevent online prompt generation from working correctly.

We do not currently use a separate website analytics service. Standard server and security logs may still record technical activity needed to operate and protect the Service.

7. Homepage advertising

Google AdSense advertising technology is loaded only on the DND Prompt Forge homepage. We do not embed the Google advertising script on guide, Privacy, Terms, About, Contact, or Excel Ratio Converter pages.

Third-party vendors, including Google, use cookies to serve ads based on a visitor's prior visits to this and other websites. Google's use of advertising cookies enables Google and its partners to serve and measure ads and, where permitted, personalize ads. Google may receive information such as the URL you visit and your IP address. Learn more about how Google uses information from sites that use its services.

You can control personalized advertising through Google Ads Settings and may opt out of certain participating companies through WebChoices. You may still receive contextual or non-personalized advertising after opting out.

8. How we disclose information

We may disclose information to:

  • Service providers. Hosting, infrastructure, security, database, and AI providers that process information for us.
  • Advertising partners. Google and its partners when you visit or interact with advertising on the homepage.
  • Legal and safety recipients. Government authorities or other parties when reasonably necessary to comply with law, respond to legal process, investigate abuse, or protect rights and safety.
  • Business transaction recipients. A successor or prospective successor in connection with a merger, acquisition, financing, reorganization, or sale of assets, subject to appropriate protections.

We do not sell personal information for money. Depending on the law that applies, homepage advertising may be considered "sharing," "targeted advertising," or a similar regulated activity.

9. Data retention

We retain information only for as long as reasonably necessary for the purposes described in this Policy, including providing the Service, maintaining security and request limits, resolving disputes, enforcing agreements, and meeting legal obligations. Retention periods vary by record type. The anonymous session cookie expires after up to 10 days; request, quota, feedback, support, and security records may be retained longer when needed for operational or legal purposes. We may delete or de-identify information when it is no longer required.

10. Data security

We use reasonable administrative and technical safeguards designed to protect information, including signed session values, access controls, and hashing certain identifiers in quota and audit records. No method of transmission or storage is completely secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

11. Your choices and privacy rights

Depending on where you live and subject to legal exceptions, you may have the right to request access to, correction of, deletion of, or a copy of personal information; to opt out of certain sales, sharing, profiling, or targeted advertising; and to appeal a denied request. We will not discriminate against you for exercising an applicable privacy right.

Describe the right you wish to exercise and provide enough information for us to locate the relevant record, such as a request ID or the email address used to contact us.
