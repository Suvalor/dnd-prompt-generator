How the DND prompt generator works

  1. Choose the prompt type: Portrait, full body, token, NPC, monster or scene — this sets the composition.
  2. Add your DND details: Race, class, a line of description, and a visual style. Open Advanced for armor, weapon and more.
  3. Copy into your image tool: Grab the main prompt and negative prompt, paste them into Midjourney, ChatGPT or any model.

Portraits, tokens, NPCs, monsters and scenes

  • Portrait: Face, costume, lighting and personality. Best for character avatars and headshots where expression carries the image.
  • Full body: Silhouette, armor, pose and weapon. Use when the whole getup matters — class fantasy and party line-ups.
  • Token: Top-down view, centered figure, readable outline. Built for virtual tabletops — clean edges and 1:1 framing.
  • NPC: Role, attitude and a memorable trait. Quick, in-world faces for the merchants, guards and patrons your party meets.
  • Monster: Anatomy, threat, scale and environment. Adds a size cue so the creature reads as dangerous at a glance.
  • Scene: Place, mood, lighting and an encounter hook. Establishing shots for locations and set-pieces, no central hero needed.

Limitations & model compatibility

DND prompt generator FAQ

What is a DND character prompt generator?

It’s a free tool that takes your character details — race, class, style, gear — and writes a structured, copy-ready text prompt you can paste into an AI image model to create portraits, tokens, NPCs, monsters or scenes.

Does this tool generate images?

No. It produces prompt text only. You copy the prompt into an image model like Midjourney or ChatGPT, which does the actual image generation.

Can I use these prompts in Midjourney or ChatGPT?

Yes. Pick your target model in Advanced and the generator tunes the wording — for Midjourney it even adds an aspect-ratio flag. The prompts also work in Gemini, Leonardo, Stable Diffusion and general-purpose tools.

Can it create top-down VTT token prompts?

Yes. Choose the Token type and the generator writes a centered, top-down figure with a clean readable outline and 1:1 framing, plus token-specific export notes for your virtual tabletop.

Is the DND prompt generator free?

Yes, it’s completely free to use, with no usage caps for the prompt generator itself.

Do I need to log in?

No. There’s no login or account required — open the page, fill the form, and copy your prompt.
